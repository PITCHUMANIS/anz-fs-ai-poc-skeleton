# Complaints Handling Procedure (Synthetic)

**doc_id:** CHP-AU-2.4  
**effective:** 2026-01-01  
**jurisdiction:** AU  
**classification:** internal-synthetic

## 2.1 Purpose

This procedure helps staff record and progress retail banking complaints consistently.
It does not authorise compensation, refunds, or goodwill payments by an AI assistant.

## 2.2 What counts as a complaint

A complaint is an expression of dissatisfaction related to a product or service, where a response
or resolution is explicitly or implicitly expected. Record the complaint even if the staff member
believes it can be resolved immediately.

## 2.3 First-day actions

1. Acknowledge the complaint and capture the customer's preferred contact method.
2. Create or update the case record with facts only — no speculative blame language.
3. Identify whether the matter is urgent (financial hardship indicators, vulnerability, legal demand).
4. If unresolved same day, provide a reference number and expected next update window.

## 2.4 Escalation to team leader

Escalate when the customer requests a fee waiver, compensation, or remediation outcome;
when vulnerability indicators are present; or when the complaint alleges serious misconduct.
AI-generated drafts must be reviewed by a human before posting to the case system.

## 2.5 Evidence and notes

Case notes must be factual, time-stamped, and free of unnecessary sensitive identifiers.
Do not paste full TFNs, full PANs, or one-time passwords into notes or prompts.
